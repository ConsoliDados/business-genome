# Skill — market-study

> **CLI:** run to fill `strategy/market.md`. **Web-chat:** paste the prompt.

**Writes:** `…/strategy/market.md`

## Steps
1. Segment definition (boundaries, verticals, B2B/B2C).
2. **Sizing**: TAM/SAM/SOM top-down + bottom-up check (# targets × deal × win-rate × frequency).
3. Demand drivers & buying behavior (triggers: cost/deadlines/value/security/brand/service).
4. **Competitor matrix** (category, pricing, strengths/weaknesses, our edge).
5. **Positioning statement** (For <target> who <need>, <product> is a <category> that <benefit>,
   unlike <alt>, because <differentiator>).
6. Trends & outlook; strategic assessment (SWOT · Porter's 5 Forces · BCG).

## Guard-rails
- Cite sources; label estimates as estimates. Don't invent market numbers.

## Prompt (copy-paste)
> Do a market study for <product>: segment definition, sizing (TAM/SAM/SOM with assumptions +
> a bottom-up check), demand drivers & buying behavior, a competitor matrix, a positioning
> statement, trends, and SWOT/Porter/BCG. Cite sources; label estimates.
